Overview Tab — Narrative Sections

The body of the Overview tab tells the full story. Each section has a bold H1 header and follows a problem → solution → proof arc. These scroll below the Reasons to Invest bullets.

The $4.3 Trillion Problem Nobody Has Fixed

Every year, American employers watch their health insurance costs rise 7-10% — doubling over a decade. They pay more, their employees get less, and the data comes 120 days late. Meanwhile, insurance company stocks have appreciated 1,600% in eight years. The system isn't broken — it's working exactly as insurance companies designed it. Just not for you.

The number of hospital administrators has grown 2,500% since 1999, while physician pay has dropped 33%. Eight percent of people account for 80% of healthcare spending, yet nobody is coordinating their care. And 60% of brokers — the people employers trust to solve this — are now owned by private equity firms with no incentive to innovate.

Employers are trapped. They know the system doesn't work, but every option looks the same.

How Vxtra Health Works — The "Our Town" Model

Vxtra Health aggregates self-funded employers within a community into a collective group — giving them the purchasing power and technology that were previously only available to Fortune 100 companies.

  • We identify a market. Secondary cities (population 50K-250K) where independent physicians thrive and everyone knows everyone. Augusta, GA is our first. Gainesville, GA is next.
  • We connect employers with the best local physicians. Independent doctors deliver the same care at 40-70% less than hospital systems — and they actually want to practice medicine, not fight pre-authorization paperwork.
  • We power it with Fortune 100 technology. Capital Rx's Judi platform is the first to process both prescription and medical claims on a single system. Employers get real-time data in 7 days.
  • We back it with world-class reinsurance. Tokyo Marine HCC provides stop-loss coverage at group rates individual employers could never access.

The result: employers get predictable, lower costs. Employees get $0 copays and $0 deductibles. Physicians get to practice medicine their way. And brokers get a product nobody else can offer.

Why Now — The Capital Rx Wave

Our technology partner Capital Rx has gone from a $12.8M pre-money valuation to $3.58B in six years. They're processing claims for Fortune 100 companies including Apple and General Motors — and they're planning an IPO in 2027.

This creates a massive awareness wave. When the world's largest employers validate a technology platform, it removes the #1 objection for mid-market employers: "Can I trust this?"

Vxtra Health is the first company to bring Capital Rx's Fortune 100 technology to the self-funded employer market of 200-2,000 employees. We're riding the wake of a $3.58B company — and we're the only boat in the water.

The Expansion Playbook — 24 Markets and Growing

Our "Our Town" model is designed to replicate. We've identified 24 markets across the Southeast and Southwest — each with the same characteristics as Augusta.

  • Phase 1 (2026): Augusta, GA launch — prove the model, collect data, build case studies
  • Phase 2 (2027): Gainesville, GA — validate replication, begin proprietary network transition
  • Phase 3 (2028+): Scale across identified markets toward 100,000+ covered lives

Each market targeting 5,000+ covered lives creates compounding underwriting advantages. At full buildout, the model projects a $1B+ valuation.

Use of Funds

  • Market Launch Operations — Employer onboarding, broker enablement, and care navigation infrastructure for Augusta (July 2026) and Gainesville (July 2027)
  • Technology Integration — Capital Rx platform setup, employer portals, and real-time data dashboards
  • Sales & Marketing — Broker recruitment, employer outreach, educational content, and LinkedIn thought leadership
  • Team Expansion — Care navigation staff, market development leads for Phase 2+ markets
  • Working Capital — Operating runway through first renewal cycle (January 2027) when recurring revenue begins compounding
Why This Narrative Structure Works Top campaigns (StartEngine's own $28.3M raise, Boxabl's $35M) follow the same arc: Problem → Model → Timing/Momentum → Expansion → Use of Funds. Each section builds on the last. The problem validates the need, the model shows the solution, timing creates urgency, expansion shows upside, and use of funds shows you have a plan. Never lead with the ask — lead with the story.

FAQ Section

Proactively addresses the top investor objections. The FAQ tab is one of the most-checked sections on StartEngine. Unanswered questions erode trust.

How does Vxtra Health make money?
Vxtra earns revenue through administrative services fees (processing claims and managing data for employers), our Vxtra Care navigation service (a personal care concierge for employees), and risk margin on the aggregated employer pool. Revenue begins when the first employer goes live in July 2026.
Isn't this just another insurance company?
No. Vxtra is NOT an insurance company. We're a health plan administrator for self-funded employers. The employers fund their own health plans — Vxtra provides the technology, physician network, care navigation, and group purchasing power that makes it work better and cost less. Catastrophic coverage is provided by Tokyo Marine HCC.
Why secondary markets? Why not launch in Atlanta or New York?
In secondary markets (population 50K-250K), the dots are closer together. Everyone knows everyone. Independent physicians are a larger share of the market. Broker relationships are deeper. And there's less competition from venture-backed health tech companies chasing major metros. This is where our model has the highest probability of success — and where employers are most underserved.
What happens if an employee needs specialized care outside the local market?
Employees can access specialized care anywhere. If someone needs Mayo Clinic, they go to Mayo Clinic. Vxtra's local physician network covers the vast majority of routine and specialty care — but we never restrict access to care a physician determines is necessary.
How is this different from a narrow network plan?
Vxtra is the opposite of a narrow network. We start with the best local physicians — the doctors your employees already know — and add Fortune 100 technology and group purchasing power on top. It's a better network, not a smaller one.
What's the relationship with Capital Rx?
Capital Rx is our technology partner. Their Judi platform is the first system to process both prescription and medical claims on a single platform. They're valued at $3.58B, serve Fortune 100 companies like Apple and General Motors, and plan to IPO in 2027. Vxtra is the first company bringing their technology to mid-market employers.
When does Vxtra expect to generate revenue?
Revenue begins July 1, 2026 when the first employers go live in Augusta, GA. We target 500 covered lives by October 2026 and 5,000+ covered lives by Q1 2027 across Augusta and Gainesville markets.

Investor Psychology — Why Vxtra Wins

Most successful StartEngine campaigns are consumer products. Vxtra is B2B — which normally underperforms. But Vxtra has a unique psychological advantage.

The Unfair Advantage Every investor is a healthcare consumer. Every one of them has dreaded opening a medical bill, fought with an insurance company, watched premiums rise, or heard a doctor complain about bureaucracy. The problem needs zero explanation. The villain is pre-established. The emotional hook is personal, not theoretical.

Critical Guidelines

Messaging Do's & Don'ts

StartEngine-specific messaging rules that combine platform best practices with Vxtra's brand guidelines and the Cigna network constraint.

✓ Do

  • Lead every section with a specific number ($4.3T, 85,000, 40-70%, 7 days vs. 120)
  • Use "physician" and "doctor" — never "provider"
  • Frame the investor as someone who experienced bad healthcare and can now fund the fix
  • Reference Capital Rx's Fortune 100 clients (Apple, GM) for borrowed credibility
  • Make the expansion playbook (24 markets) the growth and valuation story
  • Update frequently with real numbers from Augusta launch
  • Use Larry's exit track record (22x, 15x) in every credibility touchpoint
  • Answer every Discussion tab question within 24 hours
  • Use dollar amounts, not percentages ("$25/employee/month" over "25% savings")

✗ Don't

  • Don't name specific insurance companies (Cigna constraint until mid-2027) — use "traditional insurance" or "the old system"
  • Don't use jargon: no EOB, TPA, PBM, CPT, NADAC, stop-loss, or captive in investor-facing copy
  • Don't say "healthcare is broken" — say "how healthcare is paid for and administered isn't working"
  • Don't over-explain operational details — investors buy vision, not plumbing
  • Don't leave the Discussion tab unanswered for more than 24 hours
  • Don't skip updates — momentum perception is everything on StartEngine
  • Don't use high cognitive load sentences or insider baseball
  • Don't launch without a founder-led video — it's the #1 conversion driver
